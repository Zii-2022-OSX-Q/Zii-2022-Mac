## Overview

<a href="#">CLO 3D</a> has become the fashion industry standard for virtual garment development, adopted by major brands, manufacturers, and design schools worldwide. The software addresses fundamental inefficiencies in traditional apparel development - the cost and time of physical sampling, the environmental impact of material waste, and the limitations of flat pattern visualization.

The <a href="#">true-to-life simulation</a> results from sophisticated physics calculations modeling fabric behavior. Cloth simulation considers material weight, flexibility, stretch characteristics, and how these interact with gravity and body shape. The accuracy enables confident design decisions without physical validation at every stage. Years of development have refined simulation to remarkable precision.

<a href="#">Pattern development</a> within CLO follows established garment construction principles. Designers with pattern-making knowledge apply existing skills in the digital environment. The software doesn't replace pattern knowledge - it enhances it with immediate 3D feedback that was previously available only after physical sample construction.

The <a href="#">virtual fitting</a> process mimics physical sample review. Place garments on avatars and evaluate fit from every angle. Check ease distribution, style line placement, and proportion relationships. Make adjustments and see results immediately. The speed of digital iteration enables exploring more options than physical sample timelines permit.

<a href="#">Pressure mapping</a> visualization reveals fit characteristics. See where garments contact the body. Identify areas of tightness or looseness. Optimize fit for comfort and appearance. This analytical capability adds objective data to the subjective assessment of fit.

The <a href="#">animation capabilities</a> show garments in motion. Walk avatars through movement sequences to evaluate dynamic fit. See how garments behave during sitting, reaching, and other activities. Motion testing ensures designs work for real-life wear, not just static posing.

<a href="#">Print placement</a> tools position graphics and patterns precisely. See how prints wrap around body contours. Adjust placement for visual impact across size range. Digital placement replaces tedious physical sample iterations for print development.

The <a href="#">trim library</a> adds finishing details to designs. Buttons, zippers, labels, and hardware attach to garments virtually. See how trim choices affect overall design appearance. Complete designs include all elements, not just base fabric.

<a href="#">Modular design</a> approaches enable efficient range building. Create base designs with swappable components. Generate design variations by combining different elements. This modularity accelerates range development and ensures design cohesion.

The <a href="#">technical flat</a> generation automates documentation. Extract accurate flat drawings from 3D designs automatically. Include construction details and measurements. Technical documentation maintains consistency between design intent and production specifications.

<a href="#">Integration with PLM</a> systems connects CLO to enterprise workflows. Export data to product lifecycle management platforms. Maintain single sources of truth across design and production systems.

The <a href="#">sustainability benefits</a> increasingly drive CLO adoption. Reduced physical sampling means less material waste. Fewer courier shipments lower carbon footprint. Digital showrooms replace travel-intensive buyer meetings.
